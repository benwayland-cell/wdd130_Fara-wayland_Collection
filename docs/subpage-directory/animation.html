Animation

This is where there are really talented artists. They're just putting what they do up online for free. These include story-time animators, including some really small channels. There's also some telling a story and making a world in their art.

Ice Cream Sandwich

Ice Cream Sandwich is a story-time animator, but he also will sometimes ask silly questions of his audience — such as "Which video game item would you chose to: 1) survive a zombie apocalypse 2) use for every day things 3) annex Toronto" — and then animates his thoughts on people's responses.

Channel Recommended Video

Vritziestar

Vritziestar is a very small story-time animation channel that only really started not too long ago. She is an amazing artist who loves cute things and drawing cute things. A good one of hers is her video on her thoughts on bugs.

Channel Recommended: Thoughts on Bugs

Coopymations

Coopymations is another recent story-time animator that only started 5 months ago! He is a bit more chill of an animator. A good one of his is a video where he discusses a bad experience on his first job.

Channel Recommended: First Job

Luke Humphris

In Luke Humphris's animations he shows these fascinating worlds of his own creation. Many of the worlds and themes he explores are on the darker side — life after society collapsed, liminal spaces, and cosmic horror — but they always have a hopeful edge to them. There's always someone being hopeful even in inevitable death and always people rebuilding after society collapsed. His Inter-C-Zone series is an amazing trek through several fascinating liminal spaces.

Channel Recommended: Inter-C-Zone

Its ok koy

Its ok koy animates over songs — often more serious ones — while managing to tell a unique story between the lines. For example, in a cover of Aimee Carty's 2 Days into College, the song briefly mentions how the protagonist didn't want to date a boy named Colin because he didn't play guitar. Throughout the animation we watch Colin learning to play. There are several cute touches like this sprinkled throughout his videos.

Channel Recommended: 2 Days into College

Jim EdLee

Jim EdLee animates horizontal shorts that just barely miss being short-form content. He is best known for his You Can't Run Forever series, starring a silent protagonist and an insane blue guy searching for a hospital through a haunted, cryptid-filled forest — though it's often just that the blue guy owes someone money. Road Trip is a great entry point to the series.

Channel Recommended: Road Trip
