## How did you manage your development team and comunicate the tasks for your junior devs?
I managed my development team by first assigning each developer a page of the website that they would focus
on the development of and then gave small sub tasks that they should also do, such as checking links
for validity and testing if GitHub pages is displaying the website properly. Our main method of
communication was discord, but they could see their roles and tasks in GitHub issues too. The advantage
of using discord was we could quickly share information amongst the team members, and I also had created
channels to reflect the Roles.md and Assignments.md, so they could quickly look at what was expected of
them. This method proved effective and was helpful for catching mistakes and rectifying them early. I 
had made a mistake when I announced how the links for titles and banners should work, and one of the
junior devs noticed the discrepancy between the wireframe and my statement. This meant that I could 
quickly change my previously incorrect announcement and then fix the incorrect html on subpage I 
was working on.

## How did you communicate with your client? Was there any issues?
We first established how we would communicate going forward in person. After
discussing some details and questions about the site initially, like the use
of a stock image on the home page and how to get banner images for the subpages,
we decided to use discord to communicate. This worked well and when a new junior
developer was added, I was able to reach out and shortly the junior developer was
also added to the projects repository as a collaborator. There were no issues with
communication and if there was ever a question that arose, I often would ask in-person
about it. Discord was more used for when something needed to be communicated outside
of class and it was pressing or urgent, such as adding a new junior developer.

## How did you handle publishing your clients site and sharing it with the client?
The publishing and creation of the site was handled by the client, and then from the
GitHub pages deployement I took the link and posted it in the README.md file so it 
could more easily be accessed by the client and junior devs. The client had created the base 
folder structure as outlined by the assignment, and from there I created any additional files 
that we needed. Then, the development team and I would push the changes to GitHub and I would 
send the link to the page via discord.

## How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub?
GitHub works well most of the time, and I encountered no major issues with the site
deployed by the client or myself. Naturally, it usually took a couple minutes for the
site to deploy when new changes were pushed to the site, but I was always able to 
access the website and see the contents.
